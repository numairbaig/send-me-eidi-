Eidi Tradition

Eidi, also known as Eidya, is a cherished Middle Eastern Arab tradition associated with the celebration of two significant Muslim holidays: Eid al-Fitr and Eid al-Adha. Here are some key points about Eidi:

Purpose and Tradition:

  • Eidi involves gifting cash to children and family members.
  • Older relatives or family friends typically give Eidi.
  • The act of giving Eidi has historical ties to Prophet Muhammad (Peace Be Upon Him).
  • It emphasizes celebrating and rewarding children, allowing them independence and choice in selecting their gifts.

Customs and Practices:

  • Children line up from youngest to oldest in front of the oldest family member to receive their Eidi.
  • The value of the gift increases with the age of the child.
  • The last child in the line often receives the highest value gift.
  • Spouses may give jewelry, clothes, watches, perfume, or makeup.
  • Parents and in-laws may give adult children clothes or cosmetics.
  • Friends and siblings often exchange Eidi cards.
